### Core findings
- The `reprojectAllSurfaces` function is clean and self-contained (~30 lines). It iterates all walls and their surfaces, re-computes `fromCm/toCm` from the room's current `floorPosition` + `polygonVertices`, and logs when values change by >0.5cm.
- Hairpin detection uses the existing `cross < 0.01 && dot < 0` branch — just needed an explicit `else` clause instead of falling through to the `thickPrev` fallback.
- True 180° hairpins are impossible in valid non-self-intersecting polygons, but near-hairpins (179°+) can occur. The fix handles both the theoretical exact case and the near-case correctly.
- The 2x clamp for H4 is conservative — at 90° corners, the intersection formula yields exactly `thickness` (well within 2x). The corner fill system handles any residual gaps from under-extension at obtuse angles.

## Log-Driven Bug Fix: Stale roomEdge After Owner Room Deletion

**Discovered from**: Analysis of `walls_assited.log` + `asisted_mode_creation.json`

**Problem**: When a wall's owner room is deleted but the wall survives (because a guest room has a surface on it), `wall.roomEdge` still points to the deleted room. This causes `getWallNormal` to hit its `{0,-1}` fallback on every render cycle, and `enforceAdjacentPositions` uses the wrong normal to shift rooms.

**Concrete case**: Wall `0681036d` owned by deleted room `51f11de1`, with surviving surface for room `f61cb116:e5`.

**Fix**: Added ownership reassignment at the end of `pruneOrphanSurfaces`: after surfaces are filtered, if `wall.roomEdge.roomId` is no longer in `roomIds` but `wall.surfaces` is non-empty, reassign `roomEdge` to the first surviving surface's `{roomId, edgeIndex}`.

**Files**: `src/walls.js` (~6 lines), `src/walls.test.js` (+3 tests)
